--- layout: post title: 'Complex Analysis' --- ### Examples Ex $$1$$ $$ \begin{array} \LARGE \textstyle{Find all} z \in \mathbb{C} \textstyle{such that} z \cdot \|z \| + 2z + i = 0. \tag{..1.01a} \label{1.01a} \\ \textstyle{Find all} z \in \mathbb{C} \textstyle{such that} \|z \|^2 - 2i \cdot z + 2a(1+i) = 0 \textstyle{where} a \in \mathbb{R}, a \ge 0 \textstyle{is given.} \tag{..1.01a} \label{1.01a} \end{array} $$ {: style="text-align: center"} Soln: $$ \begin{array} \eqref{1.0.1a} \Large \textstyle{Put} A := \{ z \in \mathbb{C} : z \cdot \|z \| + 2z + i = 0 \} \\ \Large \textstyle{Then,} z \in A \implies (x+ iy)\sqrt[x^2 + y^2] + 2(x+iy) + i = 0 \\ \Large \textstyle{Seperate real and imaginary parts:} x(\sqrt[x^2 + y^2] + 2) + i(y\sqrt[x^2 + y^2] + 2y + 1) = 0 \\ \implies x(\sqrt[x^2 + y^2] + 2) = 0 \land (y\sqrt[x^2 + y^2] + 2y + 1) = 0 \\ \Large \textstyle{First the real part:} x(\sqrt[x^2 + y^2] + 2) = 0 \implies x=0 \lor \sqrt[x^2 + y^2] = - 2 \\ \Large \textstyle{Where} \sqrt[x^2 + y^2] \ge 0 \implies \sqrt[x^2 + y^2] \neq - 2 \textstyle{such that} x = 0 \textstyle{is our only condition} \\ \Large \textstyle{Next we consider the imaginary part:} (y\sqrt[y^2] + 2y + 1) = 0 \implies y^2 + 2y + 1 = 0 \\ \end{array} $$ {: style="text-align: center"}
